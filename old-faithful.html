old faithful

neural-network based time series prediction to model geyser eruptions

background

Old Faithful has long been known for its large geyser plume, and its frequence and regularity of eruptions. Though common lore suggests that it erupts every hour, on the hour, it actually erupts closer to every 90 minutes.
Predicting the geyser's next time of eruption is important to the Yellowstone park service as it allows visitors to get in position to watch the geyser eruption. The current forecasting algorithm for the time until next eruption is based on a simple regression based on the duration of the previous eruption. The distribution of time to next eruption is bimodal, with a peak at 65 minutes for short eruptions, and 92 minutes for long eruptions.
I visited Yellowstone in 2018, and wondered if a neural network might be able to tease out more subtle correlations in the eruption patterns to be able to forecast more accurately.

data format

Though the best dataset to use Old Faithful is closely monitored by the park service in Yellowstone with the time and rough duration of the eruption logged. The duration of the previous eruption is one of the main features used by the park to predict when the next eruption will occur. Additionally, the temperature at geyser mouth was monitored every minute from April through October of 2015. Given that temperature at the mouth of the geyser captures the duration of the eruption in the most rich and consistent of a manner, I used this dataset for this model.
